Lake Tahoe

A natural wonder at the California-Nevada border

Lake Tahoe is a large freshwater lake in the Sierra Nevada Mountains, straddling the border of California and Nevada. It’s known for its beaches and ski resorts. On the southwest shore, Emerald Bay State Park contains the 1929 Nordic-style mansion Vikingsholm. Along the lake’s northeast side, Lake Tahoe Nevada State Park includes Sand Harbor Beach and Spooner Lake, a gateway to the long-distance Tahoe Rim Trail.

Tahoe is a great vacation spot for both winter and summer activities.

Summer activities in Tahoe:

  • Water skiing
  • Hiking
  • Camping
  • Swimming
  • Fishing
  • Kayaking

Winter activities in Tahoe:

  • Skiing
  • Snowboarding
  • Sledding
  • Snowshoeing
  • Ice skating

Note: Tahoe's beautiful wilderness setting means you may encounter bears and other potentially dangerous wildlife. Plan your travels accordingly! Use bear-safe trash containers and remember to respect the environment.
